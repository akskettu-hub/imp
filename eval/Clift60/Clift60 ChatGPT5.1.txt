My Dear Brother
I received your kind letter and order the 16th of the next
day went to the Post office they told me the order was
for Plymouth Dock. I told my old Master I must go to Leus[?]
and he was very willing I should go, he told the storys that I
must go to Dock but she said I should not goe till such
time as I had put away the Clothes for it was our
washing week. We washed some on Tues day and finished
on Wednesday the 18th old(?) in time I went over to Dock.
I does(?) she(?) I showed them the order they asked my name was
Eliz[abeth] Clift. I said it was they told me to signe the order so
I did and they gave me the two Guineas I had no trouble
to get it nor any thing to say and I had a Pleasant walk
and back to Plymouth by eight o’clock. my Dear Brother I return
you my sincere thanks for this favour and I hope you have not
streightened your self to send to me for I shall recover this,
I fear, if the Lord is pleased to send me my health I have
been hear allmost five months and hope I shall this year
out for I cannot bear Changing Pleases(?) if I can help it an
there may be great alteration by that time for I little
thought that this twelve months to be in Plymouth at
this time. we are but a small family only Master Misroft(?)
and my self. we have a great deal of confinement & seld[om]
goe out side the dore from one end of the week to the
other it is very seldom I see Sister Joamma except when
I goe to Church. then I goin(?) in to see her they live just by
new Church Mr Moon & Lyns(?) seem very glad to see me
all ways speak very kind to me and my master’s allways
gives me so many times so to drink tea with my sister.
I am afraid staying so long is consuming if it do not
use me well. it is a very quiet family. I cannot get(?) to
sister is very quiet to me & I am afraid she will not get
a better Place she is Coming off next wick. I believe she is
very well as gives her kind love to you and she will
write to you as soon as she is settled. Dear Brother if you
should get any account from Brother Robert I hope you will
let me know. If he should come home I am sure she(?) would
be very got(?) welcome as he knows Joanna is dead. I suppose
I should get my a count of her & will let you know from
time to time. Brother John since I wrote to you(?) I have
he desins(?) to call upon me at Mr Lyon’s(?) house & was very kind(?) then
he is to Continue at Wisemount(?) to Lostwithiel(?) and to Celf(?) &
in return also to the farm and the hill(?) and Dunadle(?) I
am more then as not fit to be tired in that great(?)
house for an year. Dear Brother John said at the time
I said to them he thought it would be of great Dank(?)
if it was in Lostwithiel(?) very wisht then all the
Plastering year(?) the flour was falling down
the time I was at Wisemount every one says that
desin(?) to be when I was at Edwine(?) I might fill this
Paper better that only Mr an Mrs Eyre desires to be
Rememberd to you. Mr Frances an Joseph my friend Mr an Mrs
Eyre was very kind to me when I was at Bodmin they
would scarc let me goe out their house to Eat or drink if they
Cou’d help it it was very late at night when I came to
Bodmin Henry Gregor was gone to bed so that I scarce knew
when to get a bed I went up at Jacob Hoskins an mock? t(?)
him Henry said who was this(?) I said it is I she knew my voice
an Nancy Lum was shocked to let me in well you may live
me said I should be welcome in Hesle(?) then no long of staying(?)
she but in the morning when she came was so angry I
was very angry that did not let me in and I should have
up(?) she said it had been raining this two hours you should
have come down and let me in. She orders the maid to
let me sleep any other Place while I was in town an desired when
Ever I came home I would call there then in the Door ( ? ) when I
was agoing to see me thing thought I was come there to have
Tea. I drank tea twice at Priory & supper & was in Cotty(?) told
Mr I was down to see them & they said to me that I was very glad
to be married to Jenny that lives after Mr Flavields(?). The other
(line faded)
for his trial last assize at Exacter signed he is in prison(?) I wrote
an find & I do… and Dear Brother when you write a few lines you
be glad to know if you saw Brother Robert after he was confined if you
Please to give Joanna kind Love and mine to Brother Thomas and
his wife an tell him he might have left us know he was married(?)
wen(?) Robert and his wife in Catty(?) have desired to be remembered to you
time will not grant me to say no… any more at Present
But I remain your Ever Loving and affectnat Sister

Eliz Clift

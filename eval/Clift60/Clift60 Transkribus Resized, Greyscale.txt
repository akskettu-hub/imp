My Dear Brother
I received your Gind Letter and order the 16 & the next
day I went to the Host office they told me the order was
for Plymouth Dock I told my Master & must goe to Zeile
and he was very willing I should lijve he told Mestress that I
must goe to Dock but she said I should not hee till such
time as I had Rect away the Clothes for it was our
washing wick we wash once in three weeks ses fineshd
a thursdaysving by six oclock an thin I went over to Dock
Doost office I showed them the order they ask:'d my name was
Eliz Clift I said it was they told me to sine the order so
I did and they have me the two Guineas so I had no troubel
to get it nor any thing to Day and I had a pleasant walk
I was back to Plymouth by Eight oclock my Dear Brother I return
you my sincere thanks for this favour and I hope you have not
streitned your self to send to me I hope I shall recover this
a hair if the Lord is Pleased to send me my health I have

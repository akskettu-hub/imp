Dear Dan,
I received your letter, & acknowledge
that
you were very good, & generous, & so forth to write to me so soon.
Your companion in the machine is indeed an excellent man:
the Lander-hastening part of his character is rather common; but
the other, is rare ye curious. He might be said, I think, to play
a good stick; or a bad stick, if you please; for the pun will be
the same, & it will, I suppose, be nearer the truth into the
bargain. I think there would have been some humour in taking
his cane from him, while he was amusing himself, playing it
across his back a little, & giving it him again, with a Sir! S.
"beg I may'nt interrupt you." With a musical stick, one might
be said to bear a man to some tune.

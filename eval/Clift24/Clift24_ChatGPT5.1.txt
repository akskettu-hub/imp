Dear Brother this Comes with my
Cind Love to you hoping it will find you
in good health as this Leves me at Preasent
I Bless God for it I was happy to receve your
Cind Letter but I am sorry that I had
not Opurtunity to answer your First Letter
till now tho thir hath not aday Past since I wrot
Last to you but I have thought upon you an would
have wrot If I Could but the first of September
I went to Lerren to Look after the Bark an did not
return till the 2 of october an when I retund Mrs
Eyre said she was very Glad I was Come home for the
Nurs had used her very Ill an if I would Come an
wean the Child I should sleep thir a fortnight
an then I should take the Child home with me
by night an she would Pay me for it the
Child was 9 Months old when it was weaned an
three wickes an four days after she was brought to
Bead of a nother young son But it did not live
But one day it was named Jacob an they have
no Maid since the Nurs went away so I have bin
In the house since oct the 18 night an day an you may
think this is Enough to due I scarce see the fore dore
from one End of the wick to the other Sister Joanna is
Living in Lescard Pareish it is a half mile
from the town with one Mr Moon she hath a very
Good Place I have had to Letters from her she
went to her Place the 3 of Sepr But I have not wrot
to her till Last wick when she wrot to Rose Butler
to desier her to know what was be Come of me
she said she had sent three Letters to me an receved
no answer I have not seen Brother John but twice since
I went to you last the first time I saw him was the
wick of June home an the last time I saw him was
about the Medil of Novr when he disomest me
to writ a Letter for me an send it to you the next
day if he was living but I find he did not wish
I shall Return him thanks for which I see him as
he know I could not I have not hard from
Brother Thomas Since February 24 nor I have not
hard Nothing of Dear Brother Robert Since & have
troubled my self a great deal Conserning him
when I hear of the dreadful Clamityes that are
a Broad tho I am Seerting that the Loard Can
prescarve him in the midst of the Enemy
as safe as if he was home for I am Seerting
that he is afrend to all that trust in him
I am shore he hath Been a friend to us an hath
Proveded for be yond our Expectations an I am
very happy to find that The Lord is still rising
up friends for you I hope I never shall be so long with
out writing to you I Left Castract the 25 of August
an went down in the back Street in ansom to Lieut
Kitchin I have the Chamber an oncy hath the
Kitchin I give 26 Shilings + year I have not
seceft But three wickes in the room since I
took it the Hendern Williams is maried to same
Bonady John Jord remembers to you an desors
to thing if you have seen this Maurice Since he hath
been in London Mr an Mrs Gilbert an all there
family is very well I have not been to Priory
this long time for I Canat hee no place without
the Child if it had not Bien for that I should
have gone home Long a gone to have ingurind for
you are to have known what She said a count
you But I have not the time to goe out side the
door I have not been to Meeting but once this
three Months are then I had
as for Mr an Mrs Eyre atend no place of worhep
as for my part I have all wise that the Child night
an day so I Canot writ no time for she will not
Let me stay up after she in hone to Bead Dear
Another I hope you will Excuse this Scroal for I have wrot
this with the Child in my arms the talk is that
the Cassells are going on forth with they say it
will Corne from Wadbreds to Drury Foot an from
Loswettel it will be brought to half haver an
from this to town

I moust Conclud for the Bell has rung for
me to Come to bead

So I Remain your Ever Loving
an affectorot Sister till death

Eliz Clift
